Understanding the Constitution of India

The supreme law of India, laying down the framework for political principles, procedures, and powers of government institutions.

Introduction

The Constitution of India is the supreme law of the Republic of India. It lays down the framework for the country’s political system, defining the powers and responsibilities of government institutions, safeguarding fundamental rights, and outlining the principles of governance.

Historical Background

The Constitution was adopted by the Constituent Assembly on 26 November 1949 and became effective on 26 January 1950. It replaced the Government of India Act 1935 as the country's fundamental governing document, and the Dominion of India became the Republic of India.

Key Features

The Constitution declares India a sovereign, socialist, secular, and democratic republic. It assures its citizens justice, equality, and liberty, and endeavors to promote fraternity among them.

Structure

The Constitution of India contains 395 articles in 22 parts. Additional articles and parts are inserted later through various amendments. There are also 12 schedules in the Indian Constitution.

Amendments

Since its adoption, the Constitution has been amended 106 times, with the latest amendment being on 28 September 2023.
